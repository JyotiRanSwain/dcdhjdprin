Features

Everything a Print Shop Needs

From QR scanning to payment selection, PrintHub handles the full workflow without any server setup or subscriptions.

QR Code Printing

PowerCloud PrintHub auto-generates a unique QR code for your shop. Customers scan it and land directly on your upload page — no link sharing required.

Document Upload

Customers upload PDF, DOC, or DOCX files directly from their phone browser. Files go straight to your desktop — no email, no WhatsApp, no USB drives.

Full Print Options

Customers choose Black & White or Color, Single or Duplex, paper size (A4, Letter), and number of copies — before submitting the job.

Payment Method Selection

Customers declare whether they will pay by Cash or UPI when submitting. PrintHub does not process any payment — the shopkeeper collects directly.

No payment gateway needed

Live Desktop Print Queue

Every incoming job appears instantly in the desktop application with customer name, file details, print preferences, and payment method. No refresh needed.

Auto File Deletion

Uploaded documents can be automatically deleted after printing, ensuring customer privacy and keeping your local storage clean at all times.

Setup Guide

Up and Running in 6 Steps

Takes less than 10 minutes. No technical background needed.

1

Create a Free ngrok Account

Visit ngrok.com and sign up for a free account to get your tunnel credentials.

https://ngrok.com/signup
2

Copy Your Auth Token

Log in to your ngrok Dashboard and copy your personal Auth Token from the setup page.

3

Paste Token in PrintHub

Open PowerCloud PrintHub, go to Settings → Ngrok Auth Token, paste the token, and click Save.

4

Reserve Your Static Domain

In the ngrok Dashboard, go to Domains → Reserve a Static Domain and copy it.

shopprint.ngrok-free.app
5

Set Domain in PrintHub

Go to Settings → Static Domain, paste your static domain, and click Save.

6

Click Start Server

PrintHub automatically starts the local server, connects to ngrok, generates your QR code, and begins accepting customer print requests.
